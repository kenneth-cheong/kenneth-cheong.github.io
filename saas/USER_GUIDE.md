# Digimetrics — User Guide

*A plain-English guide for Tom & June.*

Welcome! Digimetrics is your all-in-one toolkit for getting found online — on Google **and** in AI answers (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity). You don't need to be technical. You type in a website or a keyword, click a button, and get a clear report or some ready-to-use content.

This guide walks you through everything in the order you'll actually use it.

---

## 1. Signing in

1. Go to the app and click **Continue with Google**.
2. Pick your Google account. That's it — no password to remember.

The first time you sign in, a **Welcome** screen pops up and asks *"What do you want to do first?"* Just pick the option that sounds like your goal (e.g. *Get more visitors*, *Check my site's health*, *Create content*). It drops you straight onto the right tool with a couple of credits' worth of free runs ready to go.

> 💡 **Tip:** There's a **Take the tour** button (the little signpost icon, top-right) any time you want a guided walkthrough of the screen you're on.

---

## 2. Credits — how the platform "charges" for work

Almost everything you do spends **credits**. Think of them like arcade tokens: each plan refills a batch every month.

- Your remaining balance is always shown in the **top bar** (e.g. `⚡ 1,240 / 2,000`). It ticks down after each run.
- After every tool finishes, the result footer tells you exactly what it cost: *"This run used 5 credits."*
- Some things are **free** (0 credits) — most notably pulling in your own Google data (Search Console, Analytics, Ads).

**Roughly what things cost:**

| Action | Credits |
|---|---|
| A caption, a schema, a content plan | 1 |
| Looking up keywords (a batch) | 1 |
| Checking a keyword's Google rank | 1 |
| Writing/optimising an article, a strategy, personas, a media plan | 5 |
| A backlinks report | 5 |
| A landing-page or ad-site analysis | 5 |
| An AI-visibility check (ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity) | 10 |
| A full forensic site audit | 50 |
| Your own Google data (Search Console / Analytics / Ads) | **0** |

If you run low, the app warns you at 80% and again at 100%, and offers you the cheaper of *top up* or *upgrade*.

You can upgrade, downgrade, change your card, or cancel any time from **Account → Manage billing** (this opens Stripe's secure portal — we never see your card details).

## 4. Finding your way around

The menu across the top:

- **Tools** — the home grid of everything you can run, grouped by category.
- **Projects** — your websites (see below).
- **Integrations** — connect your Google accounts.
- **History** — every report you've run, saved so you can reopen it.

And under the **account menu** (your avatar, top-right):

- **Account** — your plan, billing, top-ups.
- **Usage** — credits spent, by tool and by day, with a projected "run-out" date.
- **Pricing** — compare and switch plans.
- **Support** — get help (see section 11).

---

## 5. Projects — set up your website(s) first

A **Project** is just one of your websites. Setting one up means every tool already knows which site you mean, and your rank tracking + audits all hang off it.

1. Go to **Projects → New project**.
2. Enter your domain (e.g. `acme.com`) and a friendly name.
3. Use the **project selector** (top of the screen) to switch between sites if you have more than one.

Your plan decides how many projects you can have (1 on Free, up to 25 on Expert).

---

## 6. Running a tool (the basic loop)

Every tool works the same way:

1. Click a tool card on the **Tools** page.
2. Fill in the short form (usually just a website URL or a few keywords).
3. **Stuck on what to type?** Click **Try an example** — it fills the form with a sample so you can see what a result looks like.
4. Click **Run**. It spends credits and shows your report.
5. **Save / export** the result, or find it again later under **History**.

> 💡 **Simple mode:** If the tool names feel jargon-y, the app has a plainer "Simple" view — e.g. *Technical SEO Crawler* becomes **"Website health check"**, *GEO On-Page* becomes **"Get found by AI."** Hover over any unfamiliar term (CTR, Impressions, Difficulty…) for a one-line explanation.

## 8. The one-click Site Health Check

If you just want a verdict on your whole site, go to **Site Health Check** (offered on the Welcome screen, or via the *Check my site's health* goal). Enter your URL once and it runs the technical, page-quality and AI-readiness checks together, then gives you **one scored report with a prioritised fix list**. Great as a monthly "how am I doing?" snapshot. *(Needs a paid plan.)*

---

## 9. Tracking your Google rankings over time

1. Go to **Tracking** (or the *Track my Google rankings* goal).
2. Add the keywords you care about for a project.
3. The platform checks their Google position **automatically every day** and draws you a trend line, so you can see whether you're climbing.

How many keywords you can track depends on your plan (25 on Starter → 1,000 on Expert).

---

## 10. Connecting your own Google data (Integrations)

This is the sticky, useful bit — and it's **free** (0 credits). On **Pro** and up:

1. Go to **Integrations**.
2. Click **Connect** next to **Search Console**, **Google Analytics (GA4)** or **Google Ads** and approve access in the Google popup.
3. Pick which site / property / ad account you want to see.

Now you get your real **clicks, impressions, positions** (Search Console), **visitors & conversions** (GA4) and **ad spend & cost-per-result** (Google Ads) inside Digimetrics — alongside everything else.

> Search Console also lets you **inspect a URL**, **submit a sitemap**, and **ask Google to (re)index** pages. The "remove from index" and "delete sitemap" actions ask you to confirm first, because they can't be undone.

## 11. The AI assistant

There's a **chat assistant** (the chat bubble/drawer) that knows your account context. Ask it things like *"Which of my tracked keywords dropped this week?"* or *"What should I do first to rank for 'project management software'?"* Each message costs a small number of credits.

## 13. Admin (staff only)

> ℹ️ This section is **only** for staff accounts. An **Admin** link appears in the account menu *only* if your account is on the staff allowlist — regular users never see it, and the actions below are also locked down on the server side. If you don't see the link, skip this section.

The Admin console has two tabs.

### Users

A searchable table of everyone on the platform (including pending invites). For each user you can:

- **See their plan, role (Staff/Client), status (Active/Invited)** and their credit balances — Monthly, Top-up, and Total.
- **Change their plan** with the tier dropdown. ⚠️ Changing the tier **resets their monthly credit allowance** to that plan's amount. Their separately-purchased **top-up credits are left untouched.**
- **Adjust credits** with the **± Monthly** and **± Top-up** buttons. Enter an amount (use a **negative** number to deduct) and an optional reason. Every adjustment is written to the credit ledger, so it's auditable.
- **View activity** for a user (their recent runs) — this is consent-gated and the view itself is logged.
- **Create / invite a new user** with the **+ New user** button (set their email, name, role and starting tier; optionally send an invite email).

### Support tickets

The full support inbox — **every** user's tickets, not just your own. From here staff can read, **reply to**, and **close** any ticket. (Regular users only ever see their own tickets on the Support page.) Tickets left idle auto-close after a set number of days, and the owner is notified.

> **Rule of thumb:** tier and credit changes are immediate and logged. Use the *reason* field on credit adjustments so the ledger stays self-explanatory for the next person.
