=== OPENING ===
Marketing online usually means juggling a dozen tools — one for keywords, another for your Google rankings, a third for social captions. Digimetrics puts all of it in one place: twenty-seven marketing tools, plus your own Google data, in a single workspace. Let me show you around — this'll take about a minute.

=== PLATFORM TOUR (matches the live 12-beat tour, in order) ===
[1 · Welcome] This is your home screen. Everything's grouped into five areas — S.E.O., Content, A.I. Visibility, Strategy, and your own Google data. You don't have to remember that; the tour walks you through it.
[2 · Find any tool] If you know what you want, just search — type "backlinks", "captions", anything. Brand new? Flip on Simple mode and pick a goal instead of a tool, and the app picks the tool for you.
[3 · Browse by category] Or browse by category, to narrow the grid down to just S.E.O., just Content, and so on.
[4 · Every tool is a card] Every tool is a card like this. The badge shows what it costs to run — green means free. A lock means it's part of a higher plan. But here's the nice part: even locked tools give you one real preview run, so you can try before you upgrade.
[5 · Projects] A project is just one website you're working on. Pick it here once, and every tool auto-fills that site's address — and saves your results under it. No retyping.
[6 · Your credits] Most tools run on credits — think of them like tokens. Your plan tops them back up every month, and you can click here any time to see what you've used.
[7 · Connect your Google data] This is a big one. Connect your Google accounts — Search Console, Analytics, and Google Ads — in a click. Once connected, those tools cost zero credits, because it's your own data we're showing you.
[8 · History] Every report you run is saved here. Re-open it later, or re-run it with a single click.
[9 · A.I. assistant] Meet your assistant. Ask it anything — it knows your account and the product, it can explain a result to you, and it can even recommend the right tool and open it for you.
[10 · Ask about anything on screen] And here's my favourite trick. See something on screen you don't understand? Right-click it, and the assistant explains it in plain English — or tells you what to do about it. Only want one number explained? Highlight just that bit first, then right-click.
[11 · Account, billing & support] Your plan, billing, pricing, and support all live up here.
[12 · You're set] That's the whole workspace. To go deeper on any single tool, open it and hit the Tour button next to its name — it walks you through step by step, and ends on a real, live example. Two tools are free to try right now: Caption Generator, and Keyword Analysis. Perfect first runs.

=== S.E.O. ===

=== KEYWORD ANALYSIS (free · 1 credit) ===
This tells you what people actually type into Google — how popular each search is, how hard it'd be to rank, and whether they're ready to buy. Start here before writing anything.
Up top you pick a mode — here, Keyword metrics. Then drop in your keywords, and choose a country and language. That's it.
And here's the payoff: a sortable table — volume, difficulty, cost-per-click and intent for every term. "Project management software" pulls seventy-four thousand searches a month at an eight-dollar-sixty cost-per-click. Sort by the green difficulty scores to find your easiest wins.

=== RANK CHECKER (free · 1 credit) ===
Want to know exactly where you show up on Google for a keyword, in a specific country? This is it. Re-run it weekly to watch your positions move.
Add the keywords you want to track, type in your domain, and pick a location. Done.
It shows your live position for each one — here, an average position of six-point-six across five keywords, with four already on page one. "Smart goals" jumped eight spots to number eight, and there's a history chart tracking that climb over time.

=== TIME TO RANK (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This sets realistic expectations — roughly how many months a keyword will take to reach page one, based on how competitive it is.
Enter your domain and the keywords you're aiming for, with a location and language.
You get a month-by-month forecast. An easy term like "work management" might take two to three months, while "kanban board" — difficulty ninety-five — could take a year and a half to two years, even for a site as strong as Asana.

=== ANCHOR TEXT CLEANER (2 credits · ~30–150s) ===
The words you use for your links matter to Google. This audits a page for link text that's over-used, too vague, or broken.
Paste the page address and the keyword you care about.
It flags every problem — here, thirty-eight anchors found, seven flagged, three of them over-optimised and two pointing at dead pages. Each comes with a fix.

=== TECHNICAL SEO CRAWLER (2 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This scans your site for the technical things Google cares about — broken tags, missing descriptions, slow pages.
Enter your website, and optionally how many pages to crawl and how deep to go.
You get a tidy report, worst problems first — here, twenty-three issues across ten pages, three of them critical, with an average load time of one-point-eight seconds.

=== ON-PAGE OPTIMISATION (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This tells you, element by element, how to rewrite a page so it can outrank whoever's beating you right now.
Give it the page address and the keywords you want it to rank for.
It hands back specific rewrites — a stronger title, a better headline, a missing description, and a note to expand thin content from four hundred and twenty words to about nine hundred. All benchmarked against the pages currently in the top three.

=== PAGE TECHNICAL & DOMAIN ANALYSIS (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
A fast, all-in-one snapshot of any site's health — authority, links, traffic, technical signals — in one view.
Just paste a website address.
One glance tells you everything: Asana scores ninety-five for domain authority, three million backlinks from a hundred and sixteen thousand referring domains, and three hundred and sixty thousand monthly organic visitors — with a breakdown of where its rankings sit.

=== COMPETITORS IDENTIFIER (1 credit) ===
This finds who else is competing for your keywords, and how you stack up. It's the starting map for any S.E.O. plan.
Enter a keyword or a domain, with a location and language.
It lists your real rivals. For "project management software", that's Atlassian, ClickUp and Smartsheet — with how many keywords you share, and how much traffic each pulls in. Atlassian alone shares a hundred and eighty-four thousand keywords.

=== BACKLINKS EXPLORER (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
Other sites linking to you is one of Google's biggest trust signals. This audits any site's link profile — including a competitor's.
Type in a domain and choose how wide to look.
You see the full picture: Asana has three million links from a hundred and sixteen thousand referring domains, eighty-three percent of them the valuable do-follow kind — plus a list of its strongest referring sites.

=== SCHEMA GENERATOR (1 credit) ===
This builds structured data — the hidden code that earns you rich Google listings with stars, prices and F.A.Q.s. No coding; you just fill in a form.
Pick what you're describing — a business, product, or article — then fill in the details that appear.
Out comes valid, copy-paste code, with a one-click "Test in Google" button so you can confirm it works.

=== SEO STRATEGY (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This is the flagship. Tell it about your business, and it returns a complete, prioritised S.E.O. game plan — what to do, in what order.
Enter your website, a short description of what you do, your main goal, and a location.
You get ranked strategies with the top pick highlighted — here, "capture high-intent product demand", with an estimated eighteen-percent organic lift in two quarters — plus a ready-to-action plan, each step tagged by priority and effort.

=== CONTENT ===

=== CAPTION GENERATOR (free · 1 credit) ===
This writes social captions in your brand's voice, tuned to each platform. It's free on every plan — a perfect first run.
Describe the post, add your brand name, and pick the platform and tone. Everything else is optional fine-tuning.
You get several ready-to-post variations, each in its own copyable card — complete with hooks, emojis and hashtags.

=== AI CONTENT OPTIMISER (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
Write a new draft or improve existing copy — then a team of A.I. editors fact-checks and polishes it for facts, tone and S.E.O.
Choose to write or optimise, paste your content, add your target keyword, and pick how many editors to run.
Back comes a cleaner draft plus exactly what the editors fixed — here, six issues across eight QA agents: they tightened the reading level to grade seven and lifted keyword use to "good".

=== CONTENT CHECKER (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
A proof-reader on demand. It checks copy for grammar, readability, keyword use and risky claims.
Paste the text and your target keyword. You can also add brand guides or reference pages.
You get a scored report — here it caught that "you're team's" should be "your team's", fixed "a-lot", and flagged a "guaranteed cheapest" claim you can't back up.

=== CONTENT PILLAR FRAMEWORK (1 credit) ===
This maps out what to post about — the themes and angles that keep your social content cohesive, instead of random.
Describe your brand and niche, your business model, goal and audience.
You get a clean content map. For Asana, three pillars: "Work Smarter", "Inside Asana", and "The Future of Work" — each with subtopics and an angle.

=== A.I. VISIBILITY (also called G.E.O.) ===

=== AI DISCOVERY AUDIT (10 credits · ~30–150s) ===
People now ask ChatGPT and Gemini for recommendations. This checks whether those A.I. assistants actually mention and cite you when buyers ask about your category.
Enter your brand name, website and location.
Here's the answer: all four assistants name and cite Asana, with a hundred and twenty thousand brand mentions and an average position of about five in A.I. answers.

=== AI MENTIONS TRACKER (10 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This tracks how often your brand comes up across A.I. chatbots, and how you compare.
Same simple inputs — brand name, website, location.
It breaks mentions down by engine: sixty-six thousand in Google's A.I. Overviews, thirty-two thousand in ChatGPT, six thousand in Gemini. So you can see where you're strong, and where you're invisible.

=== LLMS.TXT GENERATOR (1 credit · ~30–150s) ===
There's a new file — like a menu — that tells A.I. tools how to read your site. This checks if you have one, and writes a proper one for you.
Just paste your website address; the rest is optional.
It reports your readiness — Asana's missing the file, but does allow A.I. bots — and it generates a ready-to-use llms.txt you can copy or download.

=== GEO ON-PAGE OPTIMISATION (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This rewrites a page so A.I. answers actually pick it up and quote it — the on-page side of getting found by A.I.
Give it the page, plus the exact questions you want to win — like "best project management tool for small teams" — and your brand.
It returns targeted rewrites: add a one-sentence answer up top, a comparison table, and an F.A.Q. — the structured stuff A.I. loves to quote.

=== GEO + SEO FORENSIC AUDIT (50 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This is the big one — a deep, all-in-one audit of both your traditional S.E.O. and your A.I. readiness, boiled down to a single health score and a fix list.
It only needs one thing: your website address.
Here's the verdict — a health score of eighty-two out of a hundred, strong authority, but a missing llms.txt file — with a prioritised list of fixes, each tagged by area and urgency.

=== LANDING PAGE AUDIT (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This scores a page on how well it turns visitors into customers — clarity, speed, trust and S.E.O. — with concrete fixes.
Enter the landing page address and the keyword it targets.
Here's the read — sixty-eight out of a hundred: clarity's good and the page is fast, but trust signals are weak. Each fix is spelled out.

=== PERFORMANCE MARKETING AUDIT (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===

=== SOCIAL MEDIA AUDIT (5 credits · ~30–150s) ===
This one's a bit different — it lives on its own page. It scrapes your live social profiles across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn and YouTube, then runs an A.I. analysis of your content gaps and how competitors are winning.
You point it at your profiles and competitors, and it pulls everything in automatically.
You get a combined report — a live scorecard of what's working, what's missing, and a competitor strategy you can act on.
