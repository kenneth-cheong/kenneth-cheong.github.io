# Product Manager — Turn Our Internal Marketing Tool Into a Self-Serve SaaS Product

## Project overview

We run a digital marketing agency and have built a powerful internal platform that our own team uses to deliver SEO, content, AI-search optimisation, and reporting work for clients. It currently spans two main surfaces: a multi-tool marketing dashboard and an AI client-portal chatbot.

Today the platform is **operator-first** — designed for trained specialists who know what each tool does and how to interpret the output. We want to turn it into a **self-serve ("DIY") product** that non-expert customers can sign up for, navigate on their own, and get value from without hand-holding.

We're looking for an experienced Product Manager to **own the strategy and roadmap for this internal-tool → customer-product transition** and produce the plan and specifications our development team will build against. This is a **fixed-scope, project-based engagement** with a defined set of deliverables (detailed below).

## What the platform does today (high level)

The current toolset covers a broad slice of a marketing agency's workflow, including:

- **Website & landing-page audits** — automated SEO and AI-search ("GEO") readiness scoring with prioritised recommendations.
- **Keyword & search research** — keyword discovery, density analysis, and search-results (SERP) analysis.
- **Content tools** — content-pillar planning, article optimisation, and on-page improvement suggestions (meta, headings, alt text, internal linking, readability, grammar).
- **Conversion & reporting** — conversion-rate recommendations, social-media reporting, and client-ready quote/proposal generation.
- **Backlink & campaign management** — backlink tracking and a campaign board that integrates with our project-management workflow.
- **AI client-portal chatbot** — a conversational assistant with saved conversation history and connections to common productivity tools (email, documents, calendar).
- **Admin & roles** — an admin console with role-based access.

The underlying engine is solid and battle-tested internally. The gap is **packaging, usability, onboarding, and self-service** — taking something built for experts and making it approachable, trustworthy, and sellable to customers who run it themselves.

## The challenge we need you to solve

Internal tools and customer products are different animals. We need a PM who can bridge that gap and answer questions like:

- Which tools and workflows should we expose to customers first, and which should stay internal? What's the minimum lovable product vs. the full vision?
- How do we restructure expert-oriented workflows into guided, self-serve experiences a non-specialist can complete confidently?
- What does onboarding, activation, and "first value in 10 minutes" look like for each core tool?
- How should the product be packaged and tiered (plans, limits, permissions) for self-service signup?
- What guardrails, defaults, and explanations are needed so customers trust and correctly interpret AI-generated output?
- What's the phased roadmap — sequencing, dependencies, and a realistic MVP-to-GA path?

## Scope & deliverables

We expect this project to produce a clear, build-ready plan. Concretely:

1. **Product audit & opportunity assessment** — a structured review of the existing tools, mapping each to its customer-facing potential, effort, and risk.
2. **Target users & jobs-to-be-done** — definition of the self-serve customer segments and the core jobs each tool should serve.
3. **Productisation strategy** — what to expose, what to rebuild, what to hide; MVP definition and the longer-term vision.
4. **Self-serve UX & onboarding plan** — the flows, guidance, and activation model that turn expert workflows into DIY experiences (wireframe-level or detailed flow descriptions, in collaboration with our design resource).
5. **Packaging & monetisation recommendations** — plan tiers, usage limits, and access model for self-service signup.
6. **Phased roadmap & specs** — a prioritised, sequenced roadmap plus written specifications/user stories the development team can build from.
7. **Success metrics** — the activation, retention, and conversion metrics we should instrument from day one.

(Exact deliverable format and depth can be refined together at kickoff.)

## What we're looking for

- **Proven experience taking products from concept/internal tool to launched, self-serve SaaS** — ideally B2B and including a free-trial or freemium self-signup motion.
- **Background in martech, SEO/SEM, analytics, or adjacent data-heavy tools** strongly preferred — you should be able to get up to speed on marketing workflows quickly.
- **Strong product strategy and prioritisation skills** — comfortable making MVP scoping calls and defending them.
- **Excellent UX product sense** — you think in user flows, onboarding, and reducing time-to-value, not just feature lists.
- **Experience productising AI/LLM-powered features** for non-technical end users (guardrails, trust, explainability) is a big plus.
- **Clear written communication** — you produce specs, roadmaps, and docs that engineers and designers can act on without a meeting for every question.
- Self-directed and able to drive the work with limited oversight.
